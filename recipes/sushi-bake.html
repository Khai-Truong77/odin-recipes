Sushi Bake

Return to Home

Description

This sushi bake is a creamy, layered casserole featuring seasoned sushi rice, a rich and savory salmon filling, and roasted seaweed, all topped with fresh avocado for the perfect balance of flavors and textures. Warm, comforting, and easy to serve, it's a fun twist on traditional sushi that's perfect for sharing with family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds salmon filet
  • 1 ½ teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 2 cups sushi rice, rinsed
  • 2 ¾ cups water
  • 2 teaspoons rice vinegar
  • 8 ounces imitation crab, shredded and chopped
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup Kewpie mayonnaise, divided
  • 3 tablespoons Sriracha, plus more to taste, divided
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 3 large sheets nori (roasted seaweed)
  • 2 teaspoons furikake (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons everything bagel seasoning
  • 2 teaspoons lime juice
  • 2 sliced green onions, or more to taste
  • 1 avocado, peeled, pitted, and diced

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or aluminum foil.
  2. Place the salmon on the prepared baking sheet. Sprinkle with 1/2 teaspoon salt, black pepper, lemon pepper, garlic powder, and paprika. Drizzle with olive oil.
  3. Bake until the salmon just begins to flake easily with a fork, 10 to 12 minutes. Let cool slightly, then break into small pieces. Reduce the oven temperature to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  4. Meanwhile, combine the sushi rice, water, and remaining salt in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, stir once, reduce the heat to low, and cover. Cook until the liquid is absorbed and the rice is tender, 12 to 14 minutes. Transfer to a large bowl, fluff with a fork, stir in the rice vinegar, and let cool.
  5. In a large bowl, combine the cooled salmon, imitation crab, cream cheese, 1/2 cup Kewpie mayonnaise, 1 1/2 tablespoons Sriracha, and soy sauce. Stir until well combined.
  6. Lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish. Spread the cooked rice into an even layer. Crumble the nori sheets over the rice and sprinkle with the furikake. Spread the salmon mixture evenly over the seaweed, then sprinkle with the everything bagel seasoning.
  7. Bake at 400 degrees F (200 degrees C) until the edges are lightly browned and the filling is heated through, about 25 minutes.
  8. In a small bowl, stir together the remaining 1/2 cup Kewpie mayonnaise, remaining 1 1/2 tablespoons Sriracha, and lime juice. Drizzle over the sushi bake just before serving, then garnish with sliced green onions and diced avocado.
